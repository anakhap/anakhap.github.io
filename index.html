A Little About Me

Life's short. Step out of your comfort zone and embrace the unpredictable. Close your eyes and dance to the beat of your heart. This is the motto I live every day by.

I am currently an undergraduate student studying Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. I have a passion for both technology and art, and I love to travel and experience different cultures. I believe that creativity is what fuels great ideas, inspiration, and the projects that change our world, especially in technology. Being in university has really taught me my strengths and how to use them to my advantage.

A few of my main interests include sofware/mobile development, 3D rendering, and UI/Front End Development. My main inspiration for getting into 3D animation came from watching the movie How To Train Your Dragon, which is to this day my favourite movie and in my opinion one of the greatest works of animation ever made. And now for a quote that may inspire you:

"Sometimes it is the people no one imagines anything of who do the things that no one can imagine." - Alan Turing

Interests

Software Development

There is so much to explore when it comes to development, and I love to learn. Getting experience in mobile, back-end, security, etc. are areas I would love to explore more.

Graphic Design, UI/UX

The graphical interface says a lot about the project. If it is not implemented in an aesthetically pleasing way or very user-friendly, users will not be attracted to it. Creativity is also an important aspect of this, and devloping beautiful user interfaces is something I would love to learn more in depth.

3D Rendering

3D animation is one of my greatest fascinations and a world I am constantly drawn to. There is great beauty in being able to create something so close to real-life, such as the simulation of water, or the way hair on a character's head moves. This is something I am striving to learn on my own and would love to get experience in.

Projects

  • Personal Website v.1

    February 2012

    My original personal website that I created using HTML and CSS. A little outdated, but the frist website I created, teaching myself the baby steps to web developeent and user interface design.

  • Scribbler Robot

    November 2012

    A collaborated project including a team of 6. Programmed the scribbler robot in Python . Final project including a formal report with a case study and project timelines using gant charts. Our group's main idea was to use the scribbler to help guide a blind individual to warn them of objects in the way. In order to implement this, the TeamViewer application on the Android platform was combined with the Scribbler robot, using object detection. Libraries such as Pygame was used, and Tkinter was used for event handling.

  • RPN Calculator

    February 2013

    A simple iOS application written in Objective C. Computes calculations in reverse polish notation. Developed in Xcode.

  • RunSuite (Bash Testing Script)

    May 2014

    A testing script written in Bash used to test C++ programs. It is given a set of sample inputs that would be fed as input to the C++ program, and the corresponding sample outputs. Then the script is run with the sample inputs fed into the program and desired vs. actual outputs are compared. Errors are shown in terms of files being readable, if the files exist, and if the compared outputs are different, the actual vs. desired input will be displayed to the user.

  • Quadris

    August 2014

    The first game I have every developed! Quadris is the same idea as Tetris (one of my greatest stress releivers), only difference is it's not in real time. Written in C++ in object oriented style, and using XLib for graphics.
